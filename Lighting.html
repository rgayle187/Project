Lighting

Few hobbies amplify the complex world of light to the degree as reef tanks. As many novice reefers realize, lighting is far more than an aesthetic design. This doesn’t mean you can’t appease your visual style with appropriate lighting. In fact, light design is among the most pleasurable elements of any tank setup.

3 Major Lighting Types

These are the different heading formats:

T5 high-putput

High output lamps (H.O.) and very high output lamps (V.H.O) will allow you to keep most corals thriving if you use enough of them over your aquarium. They are now commonly called T-5 lamps. Again, using a combination of lamps to get a bluer color will make your corals look their best. Some hobbyists are quite successful keeping even the more difficult to keep SPS corals including many Acropora with T-5 lamps.

LED

The compact design of LED aquarium lights is great for owners that don’t want a lot of bulky lighting around. By being so compact, you have more options on placement that still allow easy access to the tank itself for maintenance and adding water. There are some LED aquarium lights that are waterproof and can be actually hidden under water around the edge of the aquarium.

Metal Halide

MH lamps produce intense point source light that is especially good for SPS type corals as well as other corals. They do produce lots of heat and thus temperature control will be an important consideration when using these lamps.
